# BYLAWS
## Saint Paul Johnson High School Football Boosters, Inc.

**A Minnesota Nonprofit Corporation**

---

## ARTICLE 1: MEMBERSHIP

### 1.1 Membership Classes
The corporation shall have one class of voting members. Membership shall be open to any person who:
- Is at least 18 years of age
- Supports the mission of the corporation
- Pays the annual membership dues (if any)
- Completes a membership application

### 1.2 Membership Dues
The annual membership dues, if any, shall be determined by the Board of Directors and announced to members at least thirty (30) days before implementation.

### 1.3 Membership Year
The membership year shall run from January 1 through December 31 each calendar year.

### 1.4 Rights of Members
Each member in good standing shall have:
- One vote per member on each matter submitted to a vote
- The right to attend and participate in member meetings
- The right to inspect corporation records (subject to Minnesota law)
- Access to a list of members (subject to Minnesota law)

### 1.5 Member Responsibilities
Members are expected to:
- Support the mission and purpose of the corporation
- Comply with the Bylaws and policies of the corporation
- Treat other members and volunteers with respect

### 1.6 Termination of Membership
Membership is voluntary and may be terminated by:
- Written notice by the member to the Board
- Non-payment of dues after thirty (30) days written notice
- Violation of corporation policies or bylaws (upon Board action)

---

## ARTICLE 2: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### 2.1 Authority
The Board of Directors shall have full authority to manage the business and affairs of the corporation consistent with these Bylaws and Minnesota law.

### 2.2 Number and Composition
The Board of Directors shall consist of not less than three (3) and not more than nine (9) directors. The exact number shall be determined by the Board of Directors.

The Board shall consist of:
- President (or Chair)
- Vice President
- Treasurer
- Secretary
- Up to five (5) additional directors at large

### 2.3 Qualifications
All directors must be:
- At least 18 years of age
- Members of the corporation in good standing (or willing to become members)
- Committed to the mission of the corporation

### 2.4 Election of Directors
Directors shall be elected at the annual meeting of members by a majority vote of members present and voting. Terms shall be:
- Initial terms: to be staggered when possible
- Subsequent terms: one (1) year
- Maximum consecutive terms: Unlimited

### 2.5 Removal of Directors
Any director may be removed:
- For cause by a majority vote of the members at a special meeting
- Without cause by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the members
- Automatic removal for missing three (3) consecutive board meetings without excuse

### 2.6 Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board may be filled by:
- Election by the Board of Directors to serve until the next annual meeting
- Special election by the members if vacant position is President or Treasurer

### 2.7 Director Compensation
Directors serve without compensation. However, the Board may authorize reasonable reimbursement of actual and necessary expenses incurred in connection with Board duties.

---

## ARTICLE 3: BOARD MEETINGS

### 3.1 Regular Meetings
The Board of Directors shall hold regular meetings at least quarterly during the corporation year, with special sessions called as needed, at times and places to be determined by the Board.

### 3.2 Special Meetings
Special meetings of the Board may be called by the President or any two directors upon at least forty-eight (48) hours written notice to all directors.

### 3.3 Notice of Meetings
Notice of Board meetings shall be delivered by email, phone, or written notice at least five (5) business days before the meeting (except for emergency meetings).

### 3.4 Quorum
A quorum shall consist of a majority of the directors then in office.

### 3.5 Board Actions
Board actions require the affirmative vote of a majority of directors present at a meeting where a quorum is present.

### 3.6 Remote Participation
Directors may participate in Board meetings via telephone, video conference, or other electronic means if the technology permits all participants to simultaneously hear each other and participate.

### 3.7 Meeting Minutes
The Secretary shall maintain minutes of all Board meetings, including members present, actions taken, and votes recorded.

---

## ARTICLE 4: MEMBER MEETINGS

### 4.1 Annual Meeting
The corporation shall hold an annual meeting of members in September of each year for the purpose of:
- Electing directors
- Hearing reports from the Board and committees
- Transacting other business as determined by the Board

### 4.2 Special Meetings
Special meetings of members may be called by:
- The President
- The Board of Directors (by majority vote)
- Members representing at least twenty percent (20%) of voting members

### 4.3 Notice of Member Meetings
Members shall receive written notice of member meetings at least ten (10) days before the meeting, including:
- Date, time, and place of meeting
- Purpose or proposed business
- How to vote if not attending in person

### 4.4 Quorum
A quorum for member meetings shall consist of one-third (1/3) of the members in good standing entitled to vote.

### 4.5 Voting
- Each member has one vote per matter
- Voting may occur in person or by proxy (signed written authorization)
- Members may vote by email or electronic ballot if authorized by the Board

### 4.6 Meeting Minutes
The Secretary shall maintain minutes of all member meetings and make them available to members.

---

## ARTICLE 5: OFFICERS

### 5.1 Officer Titles and Duties

**President (or Chair)**
- Presides over all Board and member meetings
- Responsible for general supervision of corporation business
- Signs contracts and documents as authorized
- Serves as ex-officio member of all committees
- Ensures the corporation fulfills its mission and complies with law

**Vice President**
- Assumes duties of President in absence or incapacity
- Serves on committees as assigned
- Assumes other duties as assigned by the Board

**Secretary**
- Maintains minute books and records
- Issues notices for meetings
- Maintains list of members and directors
- Serves as custodian of corporate records
- Records and reports Board decisions

**Treasurer**
- Manages corporation funds and financial records
- Makes deposits and accounts for all funds
- Presents financial reports to the Board monthly
- Works with accountant or bookkeeper
- Ensures proper financial controls and accounting

### 5.2 Election of Officers
Officers shall be elected by the Board of Directors at the first Board meeting following the annual member meeting. Officers serve at the pleasure of the Board and may be removed at any time.

### 5.3 Term of Office
Officers shall serve one (1) year or until their successors are elected and qualify.

### 5.4 Duties Not Exclusive
The duties of officers are not exclusive. The Board may assign additional duties and responsibilities.

---

## ARTICLE 6: COMMITTEES

### 6.1 Committee Authority
The Board may establish committees to assist in managing corporation business, such as:
- Finance/Fundraising Committee
- Membership Committee
- Event Planning Committee
- Scholarship Committee
- Other committees as needed

### 6.2 Committee Composition
Committees shall be composed of at least two members (Board members or general members). Committee members shall be appointed by the Board.

### 6.3 Committee Chair
Each committee shall have a chair appointed by the Board or elected by committee members.

### 6.4 Committee Meetings
Committees shall meet as needed and shall keep minutes of meetings.

---

## ARTICLE 7: CORPORATE RECORDS

### 7.1 Required Records
The corporation shall maintain:
- Articles of Incorporation and all amendments
- Bylaws and all amendments
- Membership records and lists
- Board and member meeting minutes
- Financial records and bank statements
- Tax documents and returns
- Contracts and agreements

### 7.2 Location of Records
Corporate records shall be maintained at the principal place of business or such other location as designated by the Board.

### 7.3 Inspection Rights
Members and directors have the right to inspect corporation records consistent with Minnesota Statutes, Chapter 317A.

---

## ARTICLE 8: FINANCIAL MATTERS

### 8.1 Fiscal Year
The fiscal year of the corporation shall be January 1 through December 31.

### 8.2 Bank Accounts
The Treasurer shall:
- Open and maintain bank accounts in the corporation's name
- Require all checks and fund transfers to be signed by two authorized persons
- Maintain records of all transactions
- Reconcile accounts monthly

### 8.3 Financial Controls
- All expenditures over $250 must be approved by the Board
- The Treasurer shall present monthly financial reports to the Board
- An annual financial review or audit shall be conducted (if required by law or Board policy)

### 8.4 Fundraising
- All fundraising must be approved by the Board
- Funds raised must be used only for corporation purposes consistent with the mission
- Fundraising events must comply with all applicable laws

### 8.5 Conflict of Interest
The Board shall adopt a Conflict of Interest Policy requiring:
- Disclosure of potential conflicts
- Recusal from voting on matters involving conflicts
- Documentation of conflicts and Board action

### 8.6 Loans and Liabilities
- The corporation shall not incur debt without Board approval
- Individual members are not liable for corporation debts
- No loans may be made to directors or officers

---

## ARTICLE 9: INDEMNIFICATION

The corporation shall indemnify any director, officer, committee member, employee, or agent against claims, damages, and expenses (including attorney fees) arising from actions taken in good faith on behalf of the corporation, to the fullest extent permitted by Minnesota Statutes, Chapter 317A.

---

## ARTICLE 10: CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

### 10.1 Purpose
To protect the corporation's interests and ensure ethical governance.

### 10.2 Disclosure
Directors and officers must disclose any potential conflicts of interest:
- Before Board votes on related matters
- When hired or appointed
- When circumstances change during service

### 10.3 Recusal
Any person with a material conflict shall:
- Not participate in Board discussion of the matter
- Not vote on the matter
- Leave the room during discussion (if requested)

### 10.4 Documentation
The Secretary shall document all disclosed conflicts and Board actions taken.

---

## ARTICLE 11: AMENDMENT OF BYLAWS

### 11.1 Amendment Procedure
These Bylaws may be amended by:
- Affirmative vote of a majority of the Board of Directors, AND
- Affirmative vote of a majority of members present at a member meeting

### 11.2 Notice of Amendment
Members shall receive notice of proposed Bylaw amendments at least ten (10) days before a member vote.

---

## ARTICLE 12: DISSOLUTION

### 12.1 Dissolution Procedure
The corporation may be dissolved by:
- Majority vote of the members at a special meeting called for that purpose
- Written consent of all members

### 12.2 Distribution of Assets
Upon dissolution, after payment of all debts and obligations:
- Remaining assets shall be distributed to tax-exempt organizations under IRC Section 501(c)(3)
- Assets shall be distributed to organizations serving Saint Paul Johnson High School or Saint Paul public education

---

## ARTICLE 13: PARLIAMENTARY PROCEDURE

Board and member meetings shall be conducted according to Robert's Rules of Order (current edition) for any procedural matters not covered by these Bylaws.

---

## ARTICLE 14: EFFECTIVE DATE

These Bylaws shall become effective upon adoption by the Board of Directors.

---
